Productivity App

Background

The existing app had many users, but the scope of use was much narrower than the capabilities of the product. I was tasked with understanding the product from a user perspective. With the knowledge I obtained, I would advocate for solutions to close the gap between the company vision and user adoption, the current product and user pain points.

User Analysis

I audited and organized thousands of customer support queries. Most issues were rooted in a misunderstanding of the interaction of the two main features: A) choosing distracting sites to blocks (the Block List) and then B) scheduling lengths of time for them to be inaccessible (the Schedule).

Personas

After reading the support cues, and talking on the phone with users, I was able to understand the overarching tone of our user base. To keep their perspective as the top priority for the rest of the project, I developed two main personas and their unique jobs to be done.

Heuristic Evaluation

How did the user data match up? I analyzed our current app interface to see where we were missing the mark and causing our users heartache.

Alignment

I presented my previous findings to the team to champion their support. Prioritization was made possible by remaining true to our mission statement and the key services we provide. I received feedback to refine my observations into a unified agenda for our team.

Requirements

After establishing team buy-in, I made a list of our core requirements before I began iterating on design solutions. Our must haves in the new design:

  • Stronger connection between making block list and creating a schedule
  • Expanded curated filter list
  • Clearer form for custom URL entry
  • Consolidated scheduler
  • A walkthrough of how the product works
  • Differentiated and emphasized “block everything” setting
  • A less text-reliant interface for free version
