Saas Product Homepage

User Feedback Analysis

Upon arriving at the productivity app company, I underwent an audit of the existing customer support data. In analyzing the feedback, it became apparent that improvements could be made to the website to address the following concerns:

  • Users do not understand what the app does in general.
  • Users don’t understand the scope of the product. They do not know that they can block apps as well as websites.
  • Users are underutilizing the "custom-add feature". They are relying almost exclusively on our limited list of preset options for blocking apps and websites.

Heuristic Evaluation

The customer support inquiries made it clear that users were misunderstanding the app before they even signed up. The expectations given from the homepage was targeted as a major root of user discontent. I performed a heuristic evaluation on the homepage. Below is a high level outline of my observations.

Stakeholder Goals

I met with the team to present my findings and to understand their vision for the homepage. The website was the pillar of our marketing efforts, so improving SEO was viewed as the highest priority. Along with the user feedback issues, I would need to restructure the content hierarchy and add navigation mapping to provide access to important pages that were not yet available to visitors.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzing competitor websites and the websites of other successful B2C Saas products to identify standardized design and content elements.

Requirements

After completing the research, I gathered a list of requirements for the new homepage design:

  • A navigation bar to reflect a new and extended site map
  • A signup form that was prominent, not hidden
  • A Unique CTA color to pop out from the green and grey
  • Explanatory product images
  • An overview of product benefits and how it worked
  • A list of use cases to appeal to a broader user base and to boost SEO keywords
  • Clear, accessible download buttons for all compatible devices

The design would be a success if it increased qualified downloads and decreased the support issue topics mentioned above.
