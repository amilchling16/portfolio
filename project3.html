HOMEPAGE REDESIGN

USER FEEDBACK ANALYSIS

Upon arriving at the company, I underwent an audit of the existing customer support data. In analyzing the feedback, it became apparent that improvements could be made to the website to address the following concerns:

  • Users simply do not understand what the app does in general.
  • Users don’t understand the scope of the product. They do not know that they can block apps as well as websites.
  • Users are underutilizing the custom add feature. They are relying almost exclusively on our limited list of preset options.

HEURISTIC EVALUATION

Now knowing that the homepage was a source of user discontent, I performed a heuristic evaluation. Below is a high level outline of my observations.

STAKEHOLDER GOALS

I met with the team to present my findings and to understand their vision for the homepage. The website was the pillar of our marketing efforts, so improving SEO was viewed as the highest priority. Along with the user feedback issues, I would need to restructure the content hierarchy and add navigation mapping to link to new pages.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

I analyzing competitor websites and the websites of other successful B2C Saas products to identify standardized design and content elements.

REQUIREMENTS

After completing the research, I gathered a list of requirements for the new homepage design:

  • Navigation bar, site mapping
  • Visible signup form
  • Unique CTA color to pop out from the green and grey
  • Explanatory product images
  • A walkthrough of how the product works
  • Use case list to boost SEO keywords
  • Download buttons for all compatible devices

The design would be a success if it increased qualified downloads and decreased the support issue topics mentioned above.
